Internet

What is the Interent?

The Internet is a worldwide network of interconnected computers and devices that communicate with each other using standard communication protocols such as TCP/IP (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol). It enables users to share information, access websites, send emails, stream videos, communicate instantly, and use various online services from anywhere in the world.

History of the Internet

  1. 1960s – The Beginning

    2. The Internet began as a research project funded by the U.S. Department of Defense. A network called ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network) was developed to connect computers at different research institutions. The first successful communication between two computers occurred in 1969.

  2. 1970s – Development of TCP/IP

    3. Researchers developed the TCP/IP communication protocol, which became the standard method for transferring data between computers. This allowed different networks to communicate with one another.

  3. 1983 – Birth of the Modern Internet

    4. On January 1, 1983, ARPANET officially adopted TCP/IP. This milestone is considered the birth of the modern Internet.

  4. 1989–1991 – World Wide Web

    5. Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web (WWW). He introduced HTML (HyperText Markup Language), HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol), and web browsers, making the Internet easy to use for ordinary people.

  5. 1990s – Public Access

    6. Internet Service Providers (ISPs) began offering Internet access to the public. Popular web browsers such as Netscape Navigator and Internet Explorer made browsing the web simple, leading to the rapid growth of websites.

  6. 2000s – Broadband and Social Media

    7. Broadband Internet replaced slower dial-up connections, allowing faster access to online content. Social media, online shopping, video streaming, and cloud computing became widely used.

  7. Today

    8. Today, billions of people use the Internet every day. It supports modern technologies such as Cloud Computing, Artificial Intelligence (AI), the Internet of Things (IoT), online education, remote work, digital banking, and many other online services

How It Works

  1. A user sends a request from a computer or smartphone.
  2. The request passes through a router and an Internet Service Provider (ISP).
  3. The Internet routes the request to the destination server.
  4. The server processes the request.
  5. The requested webpage or data is returned to the user.
